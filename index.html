I am a first-generation Latina college student.

I am someone who loves adventuring and exploring new places. I love being able to see what the world has to offer and meeting new people. Establishing new friendships is something that I truly love to experience because you get to connect with new people and learn new things that you might have never known until you met this person. For this reason, volunteering has always been a passion of mine. Throughout high school, I was in band and our Associated Student Body (ASB) Leadership. Both taught me what it means to be a leader as I was the captain of the cymbals, a bass player, and planned several events for my school. I helped plan events such as the Homecoming Dance, Spirit Week, Pep rallies and much more. I love to be led as well as being a leader and want to invest my time wisely by contributing to something that is beneficial to my community.

I have always known that I want to make an impact on the people that surround me and spark change in the world. Having parents that immigrated to America and thankfully, were granted their citizenship was an amazing accomplishment for my family. However, I know there are several children suffering because their parents have been deported. There are many families who are put in detention centers under unsanitary conditions. There are several people who are being racially profiled and killed. Prisons have become human cages. With so many injustices in America and the lack of government help towards minorities, I know that law is the path I want to pursue.
